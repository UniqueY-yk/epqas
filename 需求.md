Topic: Design and Implementation of an Examination Paper Quality Analysis System Based on Microservice Architecture

1. Complete software development in accordance with the unified process of requirement analysis, software design, implementation, testing, and deployment;
2. Implement the examination paper quality analysis system;
3. Write the graduation thesis.

The main research objectives are as follows:

1. **Basic Data Management**: Including question bank and examination paper archive management, student score archive management, exam record management, and system user permission management. The system maintains the basic information of each question in the question bank (question content, question type, knowledge point, difficulty, discrimination index, etc.) and examination paper templates (paper structure, score distribution, question setter, etc.). It records detailed information of each exam, such as exam time, participating classes, number of expected examinees, number of actual examinees, etc. Student score archives store each student's exam scores and answer details for each examination. User permission management categorizes users into different roles such as administrators, question-setting teachers, classroom teachers, and students, assigning corresponding permissions. The basic data management module ensures the accuracy and completeness of the data foundation for the operation of the examination paper quality analysis system, providing reliable support for functions such as question quality analysis, paper evaluation, and review feedback.
2. **Automatic Calculation of Multi-dimensional Question and Paper Quality Indicators**: Automatically calculate various quality indicators of questions and examination papers based on exam results. For each question, the system calculates indicators such as difficulty, discrimination, reliability, and validity. Difficulty is usually measured by the correct answer rate; discrimination measures the difference in correct answer rates between high-score and low-score groups; reliability assesses the consistency and stability of the question; validity evaluates whether the question examines the predetermined knowledge and abilities. For the entire examination paper, the system calculates the average score, standard deviation, reliability, validity, coverage rate, etc. The coverage indicator measures the extent to which the examination paper covers each knowledge point, which can be calculated in combination with the requirements of the teaching syllabus. The system can also mark questions with abnormal indicators based on preset thresholds. For example, a question with excessively low difficulty (answered correctly by more than 90% of students) or excessively low discrimination (almost the same correct answer rate between high-score and low-score groups) will be flagged as abnormal. Through the calculation of multi-dimensional quality indicators, the proposition quality of questions and examination papers is comprehensively evaluated, providing a quantitative basis for subsequent improvements.
3. **Visual Quality Diagnosis and Anomaly Detection**: Present the quality indicators of questions and examination papers in an intuitive manner, and conduct anomaly detection and diagnosis. The system provides various visual charts, such as question difficulty distribution charts, discrimination scatter plots, and paper reliability trend charts. Teachers can view the paper's performance in terms of difficulty, discrimination, reliability, etc., through radar charts and compare it with the indicators of an ideal paper. The anomaly detection module automatically identifies abnormal questions or examination papers. For example, an abnormally low difficulty of a question may mean that the question is too simple, and an abnormally low discrimination of a question may mean that the question is ambiguously phrased. For detected anomalies, the system provides possible diagnostic reasons, such as "The knowledge point examined by this question is unclear" or "The option settings are unreasonable". Through visual diagnosis, teachers can quickly understand the weak links of the examination paper, clarify the aspects that need improvement, and thereby improve their proposition level.
4. **Intelligent Examination Paper Improvement Suggestion Generation**: Automatically generate specific suggestions for improving examination papers based on quality analysis results. The system provides improvement opinions based on the quality indicators of each question. For example, for a question with excessively high difficulty, it suggests "Reduce the difficulty of the question or add hints"; for a question with low discrimination, it suggests "Modify the question stem or options to enhance discrimination ability"; for knowledge points with insufficient coverage, it suggests "Supplement questions for this knowledge point in the next proposition". The system can also propose improvement strategies based on the overall performance of the examination paper, such as "The difficulty of this examination paper is relatively high; it is recommended to appropriately increase the proportion of basic questions in the next exam" or "The discrimination of certain question types is not high; consider adjusting the question types". The intelligent improvement suggestion generation module uses a rule engine and knowledge base to convert analysis results into actionable suggestions, helping teachers improve propositions in a targeted manner. These suggestions can be directly fed back to question-setting teachers or used as a reference during proposition, continuously improving examination paper quality and accumulating proposition experience.
5. **Historical Comparison and Trend Analysis**: Conduct comparative analysis and trend tracking of quality data from previous exams. The system stores the quality indicators of each examination paper, allowing teachers to view the quality change trends of previous examination papers for a specific subject or a particular question-setting teacher. For example, through line charts, teachers can check the changes in the average difficulty and discrimination of papers set by a teacher in the last 5 times, evaluating whether their proposition level is improving or declining. Historical comparison can also be used to identify periodic or structural problems. For example, if the difficulty of questions in a certain chapter is consistently high in the final exam every year, it may be necessary to adjust teaching or proposition strategies. The trend analysis module supports prediction models, such as predicting the difficulty of the current exam based on historical data. Through historical comparison and trend analysis, teachers and schools can monitor exam quality in the long term, identify potential problems and make timely adjustments, and continuously improve the scientificity and fairness of examinations.
6. **Student Answer Analysis**: Conduct in-depth analysis of students' answer situations to provide feedback for teaching. The system can generate class or individual answer analysis reports, such as the class correct answer rate for each question, distribution of wrong options, and distribution of knowledge point mastery. Teachers can see which questions are generally answered incorrectly by the class, which may mean that the knowledge point was not explained thoroughly during teaching. For individual students, the system can point out their weak knowledge points and error question sets for targeted reinforcement. The student answer analysis module can also identify abnormal answer patterns, such as selecting the same option for all questions, indicating potential cheating. Through answer analysis, teachers can understand students' learning status and adjust teaching priorities; students can also understand their own deficiencies and improve learning methods. The results of answer analysis can be combined with those of examination paper quality analysis to jointly promote the improvement of teaching and proposition.
7. **Data Security and Privacy**: Ensure the security of system data and the privacy of student scores. The system adopts strict permission control to ensure that only authorized personnel can view exam data and examination paper content. Students' scores and answer records are encrypted and access-controlled to prevent leakage. Data transmission uses HTTPS encryption to prevent interception during transmission. The system also supports data backup and audit logs to cope with unexpected situations and trace operations. The data security module complies with relevant laws and regulations (such as the Ministry of Education's data security regulations) to protect sensitive data. For example, the principle of minimization is followed when involving students' personal information. Through comprehensive security and privacy protection, the examination paper quality analysis system can exert its effectiveness without harming the interests of schools and students, and establish trust between teachers, students and the system.
